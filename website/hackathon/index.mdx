## 🌱 What Teams Build

Teams pick a real-world problem they care about — and ship a working tech solution by the end of the day. Recurring themes include:

- 🌊 **Ocean and water pollution** — beach and waterway reporting tools
- 💛 **Youth wellbeing** — apps that support mental health and connection
- 🍲 **Community food sharing** — reducing food waste and food insecurity
- 🦎 **Native species conservation** — protecting Aotearoa's taonga
- ♻️ **School sustainability** — waste audits, energy tracking, climate action

Browse the [Prompt Library](/hackathon/prompts) for ready-to-use ideas and starter prompts for each theme.

## 🏆 What's at Stake

The winning school in each region receives a prize contribution towards **new tech equipment**, jointly funded by Deloitte, AWS, and the Hynds Foundation. Catering and the day's running costs are also covered by the partners.

## 🧭 Responsible & Trustworthy AI

Kiro is a powerful AI tool, and this event is as much about **how** students use AI as **what** they build with it. We ask every team to be honest about their AI use in their pitch, verify what the AI produces before relying on it, keep personal information out of prompts, and stop and ask a mentor if anything feels off.

Students — head to the [Trustworthy AI guide](/hackathon/get-started#trustworthy-ai) on the Hackathon Day page before you start prompting. Mentors and judges, same link if you want the short version.
