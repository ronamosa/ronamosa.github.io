# Get Started: Hackathon Day

Welcome to the Tech for Good Hackathon 2026! Today you and your team are going to build something real — an app that helps solve a problem you care about. No coding experience needed. Kiro is going to do the heavy lifting while you focus on the ideas.

Here's exactly what to do, step by step.

:::tip You've Got This
Thousands of students have done this before with zero coding experience. Follow the steps, ask your mentor if you get stuck, and have fun with it.
:::

## Step 1: Open Kiro

Find the **Kiro** app on your computer and open it. It looks like a code editor with a chat panel on the side — that chat panel is where the magic happens.

If Kiro asks you to sign in, use your Google account. That's it — no passwords to remember, no accounts to create.

:::warning Kiro Won't Open?
If your computer blocks Kiro or shows a security warning, don't panic. Head to the [Troubleshooting](/hackathon/troubleshooting) page for a quick fix.
:::

## Step 2: Open Your Team's Project Folder

Your mentor or teacher will give you a project folder (it might already be on your desktop). To open it in Kiro:

1. Click **File → Open Folder** in Kiro.
2. Find the project folder and select it.
3. Click **Open**.

You should see a bunch of files appear in the left-hand panel. Don't worry about most of them — there's only one you need to read right now.

## Step 3: Read the Requirements and Find the TODOs

Look for a file called **`requirements.md`** in the left-hand panel and click on it. This file describes:

- What your app is supposed to do
- What features are already started
- **TODO** items — these are the things your team needs to build

Read through it with your team. Talk about which TODOs you want to tackle first. Pick one to start with.

:::tip Start Small
Don't try to build everything at once. Pick the simplest TODO first, get it working, then move on to the next one. Small wins add up fast.
:::

## Step 4: Open the Kiro Chat and Type Your First Prompt

Now for the fun part. Look for the chat panel on the right side of Kiro (or open it from the menu if it's hidden).

Type a message describing what you want to build. Be specific — tell Kiro exactly what you want. For example:

```text
Add a form where users can report a polluted beach. The form should
have fields for the beach name, a description of the pollution, and
a date picker.
```

Then hit **Enter** and watch what happens.

:::tip Write Like You're Texting a Friend Who's Really Good at Coding
The more detail you give Kiro, the better the result. Instead of "make a form", say what the form is for, what fields it needs, and where it should go.
:::

## Step 5: Watch Kiro Generate Specs and Code

After you send your prompt, Kiro will start working. You'll see it:

1. **Think about your request** — it figures out what needs to happen.
2. **Write or update files** — new code appears in the editor.
3. **Explain what it did** — the chat will describe the changes.

Let it finish before you do anything else. It usually takes less than a minute.

:::warning Don't Type While Kiro Is Working
Wait until Kiro finishes generating before you send another message. Interrupting it mid-flow can cause confusion. You'll know it's done when the chat stops updating.
:::

## Step 6: Preview Your App in Chrome

Time to see what you built! Your mentor can help you with this part if needed.

1. Look in the Kiro chat — it may tell you how to start the app (usually something like clicking a "Preview" button or running a command).
2. Open **Google Chrome** on your computer.
3. Go to the address Kiro gives you (it's usually something like `http://localhost:3000`).

You should see your app running in the browser. Click around and see what it does.

:::tip Nothing Showing Up?
If the browser shows a blank page or an error, check the [Troubleshooting](/hackathon/troubleshooting) page. Your mentor can also help — that's what they're here for.
:::

## Step 7: Iterate — Ask Kiro to Add Features or Fix Things

This is where it gets really fun. Now that your app is running, go back to the Kiro chat and ask for more:

- **Add a feature:** "Add a map that shows where each beach report is located."
- **Fix something:** "The date picker isn't working — it doesn't save the date when I submit the form."
- **Make it look better:** "Change the header colour to blue and make the font bigger."
- **Add another TODO:** Go back to `requirements.md`, pick the next TODO, and ask Kiro to build it.

Every time you make a change, refresh Chrome to see the update. Keep going — each round makes your app better.

:::tip The Secret to a Great Project
The teams that do best aren't the ones who try to build the most features. They're the ones who pick a few things and make them work really well. Quality over quantity.
:::

## Step 8: Stop Coding and Prepare Your Pitch

When your mentor announces **"10 minutes left"**, it's time to stop building and start preparing your pitch.

Here's what to do:

1. **Take screenshots** of your app — open it in Chrome and capture the best screens. Use your computer's screenshot tool:
   - **Mac:** Press `Cmd + Shift + 4` and drag to select an area.
   - **Windows:** Press `Win + Shift + S` and drag to select an area.
2. **Write down your key points:**
   - What problem does your app solve?
   - Who is it for?
   - What does it do? (Show your screenshots.)
   - What would you add if you had more time?
3. **Pick a presenter** — decide who on your team will do the talking (or split it up).

:::warning Don't Skip This Step
A great app with a bad pitch won't win. A good app with a great pitch can. Spend the full 10 minutes preparing — it makes a huge difference.
:::

## You're Ready

That's it — eight steps from opening Kiro to pitching your project. Remember:

- **Ask your mentor** whenever you're stuck. They're here to help.
- **Check [Troubleshooting](/hackathon/troubleshooting)** if something breaks.
- **Have fun** — this is your chance to build something that matters.

Good luck out there. Go build something awesome. 🚀
