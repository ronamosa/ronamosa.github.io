# 🎓 Hackathon Day Guide

Kia ora! Today you and your team are going to pick a real-world problem you care about, design a solution for it, build a working prototype with **Kiro** (an AI coding tool from AWS), and pitch it to a panel of judges — all in one school day.

No coding experience needed. Kiro does the heavy lifting. Your job is to bring the ideas, ask good questions, and tell a great story at the end.

:::tip You've Got This
Hundreds of rangatahi have done events like this with zero coding experience. Follow the steps, ask your mentor when you're stuck, and have fun with it.
:::

## 🗓️ Today's Plan

The day is split into stages, and each stage has a specific job. Don't worry about remembering the times — your facilitator will keep the room on track. This is just so you know what's coming.

| Time | Stage | What you're doing |
|---|---|---|
| 9.30 | 🌅 Welcome & warm-up | Intro, icebreaker, get comfy with Kiro |
| 10.00 | 🎯 Pick your problem | Choose a social/environmental issue |
| 10.10 | ❤️ Empathise | Who's affected? Build a user persona |
| 10.30 | ☕ Short break | Stretch, snack, breathe |
| 10.40 | 💡 Ideate | "How might we…?" + pick your idea |
| 11.00 | 🍕 Lunch | Refuel |
| 11.20 | 🛠️ Build with Kiro | Prototype your solution |
| 12.00 | 🧪 Test & iterate | Show a mentor, get feedback, improve |
| 12.20 | 🎤 Pitch prep & presentations | 2–3 min pitch per team + judging |
| 2.20 | 🏆 Closing & awards | Reflection, prizes, photos |

:::note The order matters more than the exact times
Your facilitator may run things slightly faster or slower than the table. Stick with whichever stage they've called.
:::

## 1️⃣ 🌅 Welcome & warm-up (9.30am)

The day kicks off with a welcome, a quick icebreaker, and a chance to get comfortable with your laptop and Kiro before you have to use it for real.

Your facilitator will run a short **scavenger hunt** — a few small tasks that help you find your way around Kiro. Just follow along; no prep needed.

While that's happening, check that Kiro is working on your team's laptop:

1. Find the **Kiro** app and open it.
2. If it asks you to sign in, click **Google** and use a personal `@gmail.com` account (not your school email).
3. Look for the **Chat Panel** on the right — that's where you'll talk to Kiro.
4. Type `Say hello` and press Enter. If Kiro replies, you're good to go.

:::tip School Gmail not working?
Some school Google accounts block third-party apps. If sign-in fails, use a personal `@gmail.com` account or ask a mentor — they've seen this before.
:::

:::warning Something not working?
If Kiro won't open, won't sign you in, or shows an error, tell your mentor straight away. The [Troubleshooting](/hackathon/troubleshooting) page has fixes for the common ones.
:::

### What is Kiro?

Kiro is like a really smart coding assistant. You tell it what you want to build in plain English, and it writes the code for you. Two ways to use it:

- **Vibe** — chat first, then build. Great for "I have a rough idea, let's see where it goes." **Recommended for today.**
- **Spec** — plan first, then build. Better for bigger projects where you want to nail the requirements before coding.

Stick with **Vibe** unless your mentor suggests otherwise.

## 2️⃣ 🎯 Pick your problem (10.00am)

Your facilitator will share a list of **social or environmental issues** you can choose from — things like plastic in the oceans, food waste, accessibility, mental wellbeing, or community connection.

As a team, pick **one** issue you genuinely care about. Don't overthink it — 10 minutes goes fast.

:::tip Pick something real
The best projects come from teams who care about the problem. If three of you are passionate about one thing and one isn't, that's fine. If none of you care about any of it, pick the one that affects your community.
:::

## 3️⃣ ❤️ Empathise (10.10am)

Before you build anything, get clear on **who you're building for**.

Your team's job in this stage:

1. Use the resources your facilitator provides (videos, articles, case studies) to learn more about the problem.
2. Build one **user persona** — a made-up but realistic person who'd use your solution.

A user persona is just a quick sketch:

- **Name & age:** *e.g. Mere, 14*
- **What they want:** *e.g. Knows which beaches near home are safe to swim at*
- **What frustrates them:** *e.g. There's no easy way to check; council websites are confusing*
- **Where they are:** *At home, on a phone, before heading out with friends*

Keep it short. The persona is a tool, not a final deliverable.

## ☕ Short break (10.30am)

Stretch. Snack. Splash water on your face.

## 4️⃣ 💡 Ideate (10.40am)

Now generate ideas — lots of them.

Use **"How Might We…"** questions to open up your thinking. For example:

> *How might we help Mere quickly check if her local beach is safe to swim at?*
> *How might we make council water-quality data easy for teenagers to use?*
> *How might we let people warn each other about dirty beaches?*

Brainstorm as many as you can in 10 minutes. **No bad ideas yet** — quantity over quality.

Then as a team, **vote** for the one you want to build. Pick the idea that's:

- Useful for your persona
- Possible to prototype in 40 minutes
- Something at least one of you is excited about

Share your top idea with the room and listen to feedback from mentors.

## 🍕 Lunch (11.00am)

Take the full break. The next stage is the busiest one of the day.

## 5️⃣ 🛠️ Build with Kiro (11.20am – 12.00pm)

You've got **40 minutes** to turn your idea into a working prototype. Stay focused — small and shippable beats big and broken.

### Open your project

Your mentor or facilitator will tell you whether to:

- **Open a starter project** — `File → Open Folder` in Kiro, then pick the folder they point you at, **or**
- **Start a new project** — Kiro can scaffold one for you from a prompt.

If you're not sure which, ask.

### Write your first prompt

Click into the **Chat Panel** on the right (where you said hello earlier). Make sure **Vibe** mode is selected.

Now describe what you want, in plain English. Be specific — tell Kiro what the app is for, what it should do, and what it should look like.

**Good prompt:**

```text
Build a simple web app that shows a list of beaches in Auckland and
lets a user mark a beach as "polluted" with a short description and
the date. Use a clean, friendly design with big buttons.
```

**Better prompt:**

```text
I'm building an app for a 14-year-old who wants to know if her local
beach is safe to swim at before she goes there. Build a single-page
web app with:
- a list of 5 example Auckland beaches
- each beach shows its name and a "Safe to swim?" status
- a button on each beach that lets the user report pollution with a
  short description and the date
- a simple, friendly design — bright colours, big text
```

The more context you give Kiro, the better the result.

:::tip See more examples
The [Prompt Library](/hackathon/prompts) has prompts for different project types — chatbots, dashboards, games, accessibility tools — copy and adapt.
:::

### Let Kiro work

After you hit Enter, Kiro will:

1. Think about what you asked for.
2. Create or update files in your project.
3. Explain what it did in the chat.

Watch it happen. **Don't type while it's working** — interrupting can confuse it. You'll know it's done when the chat stops updating.

### Preview your app

Once Kiro finishes, ask it to run the app. The simplest prompt:

```text
How do I run this app and see it in my browser?
```

Kiro will tell you what command to run (often `npm run dev`) and give you a local URL like `http://localhost:3000`. Open **Google Chrome** and paste the URL — you should see your app live.

:::warning Blank page or an error?
Don't panic. Tell your mentor or check [Troubleshooting](/hackathon/troubleshooting). Most issues are quick fixes.
:::

### Working as a team on one laptop

Only one person can type at a time, so:

- **Rotate the keyboard** every 10–15 minutes so everyone gets a turn.
- The person at the keyboard is the **driver**. Everyone else is a **navigator** — watching, suggesting, catching mistakes.
- If you're not at the keyboard, you're not idle — you're thinking about the *next* prompt, the persona, or the pitch.

## 6️⃣ 🧪 Test & iterate (12.00pm – 12.20pm)

Show your prototype to a mentor and get honest feedback. Ask:

- Does this make sense to you?
- Would the persona we built use this?
- What's the most confusing thing about it?

Then go back to Kiro and **iterate**:

- **Add a feature:** *"Add a map showing where each beach is."*
- **Fix something:** *"The pollution date isn't saving — fix that."*
- **Polish the look:** *"Make the heading bigger and use ocean blue as the main colour."*

Refresh Chrome after each change to see updates.

:::tip When Kiro gets it wrong
Kiro isn't perfect. If something breaks or it builds the wrong thing:

1. **Tell Kiro what's wrong** in plain English — *"That button doesn't work when I click it. Fix it."*
2. **Try once more** with a clearer prompt.
3. **If it's still broken after 2 tries**, ask a mentor. Don't burn 15 minutes wrestling with the same bug.
:::

## 7️⃣ 🎤 Pitch prep & presentations (12.20pm onwards)

When the facilitator calls **"stop building, start prepping"**, close Kiro and switch into pitch mode. You'll get a **pitch template** to fill in. The pitch itself is **2–3 minutes per team**.

### What to include in your pitch

A great pitch tells a story in this order:

1. **The problem** — what's wrong, who it affects (introduce your persona).
2. **Your solution** — what you built and why it helps.
3. **A demo** — show it live in Chrome if you can. **Live demo beats screenshots every time.**
4. **What's next** — what you'd build if you had more time.

### Pitch tips

- **Tell a story, don't list features.** Start with Mere, not your code.
- **Pick a presenter (or two)** — split the talking, but don't all crowd the laptop.
- **Have a backup.** If the live demo breaks, take screenshots beforehand as a safety net:
  - **Mac:** `Cmd + Shift + 4` and drag.
  - **Windows:** `Win + Shift + S` and drag.
- **Practice once.** Say it out loud before you go up — even just to each other.

:::warning A great app with a bad pitch won't win
A good app with a great pitch absolutely can. Spend the prep time on the story, not on adding one more feature.
:::

## ✅ You're Ready

That's the day. Stages, not steps — the design thinking flows into the build, the build flows into the pitch, and your team brings the heart.

A few things to remember:

- **Ask your mentor** whenever you're stuck. They're here for you.
- **Ship something small** rather than nothing big.
- **Tell a story** at the end — judges remember stories, not features.
- **Have fun** — you're building something that could actually help someone.

Kia kaha. Go build something awesome. 🚀
